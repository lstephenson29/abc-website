Core Architecture

Bipolar Architecture Advantage

How Bipolar Lead Differs

Traditional monopolar batteries connect cells externally using heavy bus bars and complex wiring. Each cell is a separate unit, leading to uneven current distribution, wasted space, and increased resistance. ABC's bipolar architecture stacks cells internally. Each electrode serves as both the positive plate of one cell and the negative plate of the next.

This design eliminates intercell connections, reduces internal resistance, and creates a compact, high-voltage module that outperforms monopolar designs in every metric that matters for critical infrastructure.

  • Uniform current density across the entire electrode surface
  • Higher energy density through elimination of intercell hardware
  • Simpler BMS requirements due to inherent cell balancing
  • Lower manufacturing cost via automated stacking processes
